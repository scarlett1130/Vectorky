OUR HISTORY

For over 20 years, Vector Engineers Inc. has spearheaded engineering solutions that are rooted in experience, expertise, and passion. Initially specializing in civil engineering and land surveying, we are run by a dedicated team of engineers, geologists, technicians, and drillers focused the firm to specialize in geotechnical engineering, geotechnical drilling, geosciences, construction services, and forensic geotechnical engineering.

Having served Kentucky, Tennessee, and Southern Indiana for almost two decades, Vector Engineers is the preferred partner for owners, construction managers, and property developers in the three states we operate in.

OUR FUTURE (MERGER WITH CTL)

With a view of expanding its own operations across the states that Vector Engineers has a firm presence in, we acquired by CTL Engineering Inc.

CTL is a full-service consulting engineering firm serving 13 cities across Ohio, Virginia, West Virginia, Pennsylvania, Indiana, and Kentucky, in addition to an office in India. Being an employee-owned firm, CTL provides services that encompass engineering, forensic science, construction, testing, and IT. Their array of services has benefitted clients across construction and manufacturing industries, telecommunication companies, and the energy sector. Post the merger, both organizations are primed for growth with teams in place to offer even more value to customers.

Leadership

Team

With decades of experience in geotechnical engineering and construction services, our leadership team is the envy of every other engineering firm in the United States of America.

Wayne A. Karem,

Ph.D., P.E., P.G., D. GE

Having graduated with a Ph. D from the University of Kentucky, Dr. Karem brings with him over 35 years of experience in Geotechnical Engineering and Construction Materials Testing. During his illustrious career, he has been a part-time professor at the University of Kentucky between 2004-2015 and a member of the Kentucky Association of Economic Developers (KAED). Being a certified expert in Geotechnical Engineering with several published research papers to his credit, he is an integral part of the team and its deliverables. Dr. Karem was the Executive Vice President for QORE Property Sciences (Formerly Atlanta Testing and Engineering), prior to joining Vector.
Wayne is the President at Vector and is currently an advisor to the Civil Engineering Department at Western Kentucky University and an adjunct Professor at the University of Kentucky. Dr. Karem also is active in the American Council of Engineering Consultants (ACEC).

EMPLOYEE STOCK OWNERSHIP
PROGRAM (ESOP s)

Vector Engineers is part of a very small group of companies globally that are active participants in an Employee Stock Ownership Plan or ESOPs. ESOPs, an employee retirement plan governed by the Employment Retirement and Security Act (ERISA), allow employees to own stocks of the company, which they can choose to either retain or liquidate, post a certain age and tenure within the organization.

Stock option programs are fairly common in senior white-collar jobs, but Vector is one of a rare few that opened this program up to its entire workforce. This program is significant because it gives the employees a vested interest in how the company performs. Over time, this interest will develop a sense of ownership within the workforce and result in higher quality work and employee satisfaction levels.

THE CORE PRINCIPLES OF VECTOR'S STOCK OWNERSHIP PROGRAM ARE AS FOLLOWS

REALIZE

Realize that you, the employee, are a true owner of the company. And, as an owner, you have a certain responsibility to both the company you own and the employees that own it with you.

WORK HARD

Work hard, minimize waste, eliminate errors, and keep a safe workplace. Doing just this much every day is enough to make a difference.

WHATEVER YOU DO

Everything you do affects the ESOPs, so make sure whatever you do is the best that you can.

ORGANIZATION

No one individual can make a huge difference. Only together can we grow the value of the organization and your stake in it.
